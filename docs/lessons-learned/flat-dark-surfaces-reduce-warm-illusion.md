# Flat dark surfaces reduce warm illusion

When a dark theme already uses neutral grey values but still looks brownish, the problem is often not hue. It is surface treatment.

In Trama, the strongest warm illusion came from layered dark gradients, shadows, and permanently filled sidebar rows/buttons sitting next to flatter editor surfaces. Even when the sampled colors were neutral greys, the extra depth cues made the left-side chrome read as warmer and "coffee" tinted.

For future dark-theme work: before shifting greys toward blue, first flatten the major shell surfaces. Solid fills, lighter borders, and transparent inactive rows usually get much closer to VS Code's neutral look than repeatedly tweaking hex values.
