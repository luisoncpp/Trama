# Chapter 2 — The Road

**Arc:** The Call
**Dramatic question:** Can three people with nothing in common form a
working group?

---

## Opening

The north road, two hours past the city gate. Cael is on foot. The road is
straight and unremarkable. Then a cart catches up to him, and everything
starts.

---

## Scenes

**Maren.**
Her cart is loaded with considerable precision: a disassembled brick oven
in two sections, two sacks of flour in waterproof wrapping, a toolkit in a
canvas roll, provisions organized by type and shelf life. She is a woman in
her late thirties who assesses Cael in roughly four seconds and offers him a
place on the cart in exchange for help with difficult road conditions. She is
not warm about the offer. She is practical.

They do not discuss the quest. She knows about it — the king's summons were
public enough that his abrupt disappearance from the Gilded Post was noted
in certain circles — but she does not ask. She is going north for her own
reasons. He is going north for his. The cart has room.

**Oswin.**
Midday. He has been gaining on them for an hour and finally reaches the cart
at a slight run, carrying a satchel that suggests he has never successfully
prioritized when packing. He presents his credentials with the formality of
someone who has rehearsed this introduction and is relieved to be using it.

He asks if the quest has officially begun or if he has missed anything.

Maren says it has not. Cael says he is not sure it ever started.

Oswin writes both of these responses in his journal, with timestamps.

**The day's travel.**
The three of them establish, without agreeing to it, a working pattern. Maren
handles the practical logistics of the road. Oswin records things. Cael
watches both of them and says very little, which means he has already catalogued
a great deal about both of them and shared none of it.

By late afternoon there is a comfortable enough silence that no one feels the
need to fill it, which is progress of a sort.

**The crossroads inn.**
A mid-road stop, the kind that exists because travelers need it. Common room,
a fire, a meal. Oswin, who has been waiting to have this conversation since he
caught up to the cart, begins summarizing the known history of the three relics
from the original manuscript. He has the text nearly memorized. He is precise
and thorough and clearly pleased to be using his preparation.

Maren listens, then asks which version he's quoting. She once heard a completely
different account from a traveling storyteller at her town market — the relics
were in a different order, the trials were described as external rather than
internal, and the ending involved considerably more blood. Oswin says there is
only one original manuscript. Maren says that storyteller was very confident.

Cael, without looking up from his bowl, says there are at least four distinct
versions in circulation and the oldest one he knows doesn't include the relics
at all. It ends differently. He recites two lines from memory — the opening of
*The Weight of Three*, though he does not name the song. They sound like
pleasant but somewhat imprecise verse. Nothing special about them at all.

Oswin writes them down immediately. He stares at what he has written for a
moment, then draws a small box around the lines and adds a note: *these lines
do not appear in the manuscript version. Not sure why this bothers me.*

---

## Ending Beat

Nobody says anything for a moment. The fire pops. Outside, the road is dark
and quiet. Three people who have known each other for approximately ten hours
sit with a small, unexplained discrepancy between one version of a legend and
another.

The moment passes. They go to their rooms.

They are not yet a team. They are three people going the same direction who
have agreed, so far, not to make things harder for each other. This is a
reasonable beginning.

---

## What This Chapter Establishes

- The group dynamic: Maren as practical anchor, Oswin as recorder and
  organizer of knowledge, Cael as the observer who doesn't share his
  observations.
- The multiple versions of the legend: already the story is less stable
  than anyone assumed.
- Oswin's method: he documents everything, including things he does not yet
  understand. This habit will matter enormously later.

## Plant

The two lines Cael recites — the opening of *The Weight of Three* — go into
Oswin's journal with a box around them. This is the first of three times
Oswin will mark something related to that song. Each marking is done without
understanding. All three will converge in Chapter 10.
