# Valdenmere — The Capital

## Overview

Valdenmere is the largest city in the kingdom and its administrative center. It sits on flat ground at the confluence of two rivers, which made it a natural trading hub long before it became a seat of government. The city is old enough that its oldest districts no longer remember what they were built for. Streets follow paths that were cart tracks a thousand years ago.

It is not a city of particular beauty. It is a city of accumulated function — walls added to walls, districts absorbed over decades, neighborhoods that were once independent villages and still act like it. The palace is the exception: it was built to impress, and it does, but it impresses from a distance better than it does up close.

## Relevant Details

**The palace** occupies the northern quarter of the city's interior ring. It is older than the current dynasty but has been renovated so many times that almost nothing of the original structure remains visible. The throne room is large and deliberately cold-feeling. The small receiving rooms used for private audiences are warmer but not comfortable.

**The Gilded Post**, the inn where Cael is performing when the summons arrives, is in the trades district — not the worst part of the city, not the best. It has twenty rooms, a common room that fills on market nights, and a landlord who charges double for performers who draw a crowd and triple for performers who don't. Cael is on the triple rate.

**The north gate** is the departure point for the road to Drest and the northern territories. It opens at dawn and closes at dusk except during trade season. The guards at the gate are neither suspicious nor interested. They see fifty people leave every morning.

## The King's Position

The king has ruled Valdenmere for twenty-three years. He is, by most accounts, a competent administrator who has kept the kingdom stable and the trade routes functional. His public grief over the "death" of his son — murdered by the Demon King, the story goes — is one of the defining facts of his reign. People pity him for it. The pity keeps him sympathetic.

He does not appear in the story after the opening chapter except by implication, until the aftermath of the resolution.

## Atmosphere

Valdenmere does not feel like a city waiting for something. It feels like a city that has already decided what is true and is getting on with things. Rumors exist, of course — they always do — but the official narrative is deeply embedded. The prince has been dead for years. The Demon King is a monster at the border. The relics are a legend. Everything is being handled.

Cael grew up two days from here and has spent years performing in the city without once feeling like he belonged to it. He leaves without saying goodbye to anyone, which is not unusual. He has never stayed long enough to have anyone to say goodbye to.
